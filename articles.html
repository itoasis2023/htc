    Baobab Oil


    The Baoba Tree(Adansonia digitata) has captivated the people since time immemorial, for it can live to an age of 500 ears or more without problems and reach a height of 20m and a trunk diameter or 12m in the native African tree savannah. "Magic Tree", "chemist's tree", "tree of life": the designations of the local inhibitants for the baobab shows how important it its life in the challenging African environment. The fruit is naturally dry in ripe condition and contains only 8-9% of water when harvested. Seeds, bark, leaves, and shoots deliver several irreplaceable active ingredients and thus are used in a variety of fields, e.g. for food, tradional medicine, and cosmetics.

    Due to its versatile properties, baobab oil is particular interest in cosmetics. It is characterised by its antioxidant properties and its high content of palmitic acid, which, in turn, shows a remarkable affinity to the skin, thus having a positive impact on the skin penetration. In fact, a study of Ferrara University proves that local application of baobab oil significantly increases skin elasticity. Its high content of tocopherols results in considerable oxidative stability. The Baobab Oxy-Oil is also extremely effective as free radical scavenger. Moreover, it inhibits the growth of dermatophytes and Candida albicans, which makes it a meaningful additive to be used in hand care and foot care products (particularly as far as the nails are concerned). In addition, both oils give new brilliance to dry hair.

    Dulse Red Algae


    Dulse seaweed is full of lipids, proteins, minerals and vitamins that are readily absorbed into the skin. Upon absorbption, Dulse rebalances, nourishes, detoxifies, purifies, oxygenates and deeply moisturizes the skin's texture and tone. Dulse is also an excellent source of amino acids, as well as vitamins A, C, B1, B12, D, E, K & P - thereby making it ideal for forcing toxins from stagnant cells.

    The use of Dulse seaweed also stimulates and improves blood and lymph circulation, while combating premature aging, environmental stressors and the impact of poor nutrition and pollution - all of which take a terrible toll on the skin. What's more, the seawater in Dulse seaweed is very similar to human plasma, thus making it ideal for cleansing, toning and soothing human skin - all while regenerating broken capillaries and the skin's tissue, to render a more youthful appearance.

    W3 Peptide (HEXAPEPTIDE - 33)


    W3 - Hexapeptide, Nobel Prize winning ingredient W3 Peptide is the novel Skin Care Peptide of the 3rd Generation targetting "G-Protein-Coupled Receptor", which makes it work on the skin from the inside out.

    Peptides are molecules that contain two or more amino acids linked by peptide bonds. Their nomeclature is derived from the number of amino acids in the chain. For example, dipeptides contain two amino acids ; tripeptides three; tetrapeptides four, etc.

    Peptides play an important part in rebuilding collagen, which degrades as we age resulting in sagging and wrinkled skin. Because of this function, these molecules are heralded in the anti-aging market with claims such as:

    * Minimizes fine lines and wrinkles.

    * Improves skin elasticity.

    * Activates collagen production, stimulates, repair and renewal mechanisms of the skin.
